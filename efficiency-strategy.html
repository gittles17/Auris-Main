THE METHODOLOGY

Start with the data. Build from what it shows.

We pulled every hour logged across the company for the full fiscal year and cross-referenced it against the P&L, line by line. The goal: understand exactly where time goes, where processes create drag, and where technology can accelerate the workflow.

The analysis mapped every non-billable hour to a cost pool, then scored each pool on three dimensions: size of the opportunity, feasibility of automation, and confidence level based on existing tooling. Six areas emerged as clear, high-confidence targets.

THE APPROACH

Infrastructure first. Then scale on it.

The efficiency analysis identifies the opportunities. The development plan sequences them so each layer builds on the one before it.

Layer 1

Architecture and deployment

A centralized tool hub, automated deployment pipeline, and security-scanned distribution. The structural layer that every other initiative depends on.

Layer 2

High-confidence automation

AI-powered media analysis, intelligent search, predictive scheduling. Proven capabilities with clear integration paths and immediate time savings.

Layer 3

Workflow transformation

Producer workflow automation and finishing pipeline scripting. The two largest efficiency opportunities, rolled out department by department on top of the platform and adoption built in earlier layers.

Layer 4

Measure and repeat

Adoption data feeds back into the analysis. What works scales. What doesn't gets pivoted or retired. The next layer of opportunity surfaces from a higher baseline.

THE INVESTMENT

Low cost. High return. Built in-house.

Total external licensing runs approximately $30K per year. Everything else is built internally using open-source and commercial AI models, chosen by use case.

Effective total investment, including internal engineering time, runs approximately $90-105K per year. Against an addressable improvement of over $1M annually, that represents a 10-17x return.

Tools are deployed and adoption is expanding across departments. Each wave of tooling funds the next.

THE CYCLE

Continuous, not one-time.

Each quarter, as new tools ship and workflows evolve, the data surfaces the next layer of opportunity. What was manual becomes automated. What was estimated becomes measured. The operational baseline keeps rising.

Systematically identifying friction, building targeted solutions, measuring the impact, cycling again. Technology is the accelerant. The discipline is the differentiator.
