THE CHALLENGE

The Model Is Breaking

Studios are consolidating. Streaming growth is decelerating. Gaming budgets rival theatrical, but publishers expect full creative support across global launches, live service, and franchise campaigns at the same time. Budgets shrink. Scope expands. The studios that survive sell judgment, not hours. The ones that try to scale hit the same two walls.

THE LINEAR TRAP

More work means more people. More people means more overhead. Revenue grows, margins don't.

INFORMATION SILOS

Information lives in a dozen platforms. Teams spend time chasing data instead of making creative decisions.

THE SOLUTION

Create's proprietary intelligence layer. Platforms, dashboards, and automated systems that connect every part of the studio. Private and on-premise where client IP requires it. Open-source and commercial AI models, chosen by use case. 20+ tools deployed in a single quarter. Operational IP that comes with the company.

WHAT WE'VE BUILT

20+ tools. One quarter. Built from the inside out.

Proprietary IP that scales with revenue.

The legacy tech stack was stripped down and rebuilt from scratch. Every tool is purpose-built for this studio. But the bigger shift is cultural. Every person has enterprise AI access, and they use it. Producers reduce bottlenecks. Coordinators build intake tools. Creative directors create better workflows for their teams. The studio did not just adopt AI. It created builders. That advantage compounds.

Auris Intelligence

Auris Markets

Producers and creative directors used to go into client meetings without knowing what else was on the slate or what competitors were doing. Auris Markets solves that.

It tracks release slates, franchise launches, audience trends, and competitive positioning across film, series, and gaming, and matches them to the client portfolio automatically. AI chat, pipeline tracking, branded client presentations.

22 active users 255 sessions / 90 days

Auris Weekly Newsletter

Everyone in the studio contributes to winning business, not just the sales team. Every week, this newsletter lands in every inbox across the company.

It scans industry signals, casting announcements, development news, greenlights, and matches them to the portfolio automatically. Anyone can spot an opportunity and act on it.

172 recipients 58% open rate

Auris Storyboards

Storyboarding was one of the most time-intensive steps in pre-production, and most of that time was spent on frames that would change.

AI storyboard generation from scene descriptions and briefs now handles that across AV, social, and gaming. Runs on-prem for IP security.

458 hrs saved / yr $35K freelance eliminated

Cue sheets are tedious, manual, and legally required. The studio spent 972 hours on them in 2025.

This tool ingests Premiere project files and extracts music usage data automatically.

72% automated 972 hrs addressed

The Culture Shift

Any studio can license AI. Getting 150 people to trust it, build with it voluntarily, and see it as amplification rather than replacement is harder than building the software. That already happened here.

No one was told to adopt AI. The approach was built on earning trust. Tools were designed alongside the people who use them. Field experts lead their areas, and the dev team helps them build what they could not build before. The result: adoption that sticks because it was never forced.

Producers, coordinators, and creative directors build their own tools and share what works across departments. The mentality spread without being mandated. People who were skeptical six months ago now prototype solutions for their own teams.

Each tool makes the next one faster to build and easier to adopt. A knowledge system prevents tribal knowledge loss. An "Agent Tennis" co-development protocol lets domain experts co-build tools with the dev team. One developer built 20+ tools with no contractors.
