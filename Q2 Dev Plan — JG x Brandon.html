CREATE / Q2 DEVELOPMENT PLAN

From prototyping to production.

The team is literate. The tools work. Now we build the house they live in. This quarter is about architecture, activation, and expanding beyond the design group.

01 — THE SHIFT

Stop tightening the waistband. Start moving faster.

Q1 was about getting people comfortable. It worked. The design group builds daily, other departments are watching and starting to participate, and the "yes, and" culture is taking hold — Marcel, Mike, Casey, and others are picking up tools and riffing without being asked.

The next move isn't more prototyping. It's putting a structure around everything so the studio can see what exists, where it's going, and how they're a part of it. One front door. Clear suites. A way for people to contribute without it turning into chaos.

The thinking also shifts. We're done looking backward at what we can trim. The question now is: how does this studio work bigger, move faster, and do things that weren't possible six months ago?

02 — STRUCTURE

Two suites. One front door.

Everything consolidates under create.work, a single internal destination where the studio goes to find tools, see development status, and contribute their own work. Both suites below sit under the broader Auris development umbrella.

Auris
AI-generated and AI-intelligence tools. Anything with a generative or intelligence layer qualifies — this is the capex classification. Markets, Storyboards, Cue Sheets, image-to-3D, music search, text-to-image.
Machine Shop
Production infrastructure. Scripts, plugins, automation. Launcher, LoadBalance, Auto_Hours, AE Pipeline, CFO Reports, Shotmap, production packet tools. The diesel that keeps the studio running.

A community layer lets the team upload their own tools. IT scans for security. If it's clean, it goes live. The deployment model moves to GitHub with automated builds and security scanning — less burden on Steve's team, more self-service without sacrificing safety.

Brandon's first deliverable is the architecture deck that maps all of this: the two suites, deployment flow, how the studio participates, where everything lives. Until that's locked, most other development pauses.

04 — RISKS

Three things that slow us down.

IT bandwidth. Steve's team is buried and can't support the deployment volume. The GitHub pipeline is the structural fix, but it needs to be treated as a priority. JG has the direct conversation by end of April.

FileMaker + editorial resistance. Auto_Hours is blocked by FileMaker's hostile API and the editorial team's distrust. Different strategy needed — force the adoption conversation and evaluate whether Notion can absorb the function long-term.

Adoption habits. People still default to what they know. The tool hub is the structural answer, but it only works with consistent reinforcement and engagement tracking. If something isn't getting used, we need to know why — and decide whether it needs a pivot or just more time.

THE REAL VARIABLE

Trust. The energy is in a different place than it was six months ago. People are comfortable, even excited. The posture that got us here stays the same: field experts lead their areas, dev team helps them build what they couldn't build before. Be genuine, be direct, come correct. That's the whole thing.
